Why I joined DAAA

I originally planned to do Computing in Junior College (JC) however, I realised that the JC life did not sound appealing; There was no point in learning things I would not apply in the future. So I broke down what I loved about coding and stumbled across Applied AI in Singapore Polytechnic the choice was a no-brainer and I have not looked back since.

New Environment

Although it seemed daunting at first, being a people-oriented person certainly helped me in making new friends in my course. Adjusting to the new work load of Polytechnic is also difficult as it is very different from secondary school. In poly, you need self-discipline and be able to manage yourself to be able to succeed.

As you can see, we have many different modules in DAAA, not to mention there are group projects and individual projects (like this website!). These projects take a long time to complete and you really need to plan out your days to make the most of your time. Especially when it comes to group projects where your marks are determined by you and your group-mates efforts so good communication skills and being proactive certainly helps you on the quest to a good GPA.
